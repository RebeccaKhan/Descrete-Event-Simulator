Task 4.4 : Analysis and General Questions¶

Task 4.4.1: Lag size¶

The given two sequences in the Task 4.2.1 repeat themselves after every second and third element respectively. Hence a lag size of 0 and 1 for the first sequence and 0,1 and 2 for the second sequence are sufficent. Just to demonstrate the cyclic nature of the the sequence and the correlation, I have increased the maximum lag to 4 and 6 respectively.

The correlation coefficient measures the strength of the relationship between the relative movements of two sequences, hence when the lag size is equal to the number of elements after which the sequence repeats itself, then we see the correlation as 1 such as for a lag size of 0. For sequence 1, for a lag of 1 we see a correlation of -1 because the lagged sequence is just the inverted original sequence.

Task 4.4.2: Correlation Times in the DES¶

  1. IAT and Waiting time of a packet : The correlation values are observed to be negative, which means that if the packets arrive close to each other, they have to wait longer. However, the correlation values are small because of the general randomness of our service time as well.

  2. IAT and Service time of a packet: The correlation values are close to 0 ( both positive and negative) and this is becuse both the service time and IAT is generated with our two RNGs.

  3. IAT and System Time of a packet: These values are observed to be low and negative(just as IAT and waiting time). However these values will always be lower than the correlaiton between IAT and waiting time since System Time is just the sum of waiting time and service time. We have reasoned why IAT and waiting time have a negative correlation and IAT and Service time have not correlation, IAT and service time will also have negative and less correlation.

  4. Service Time and System Time: The correlation between the service time and system time are observed to be very high for lower RHO values (0.01 and 0.5) this is an expected result as packets as System time is the sum of Service time and waiting time, and for lower RHO values the waiting time of the packets is very low and hence the System time is effectively just the service time. However, as the we increase RHO we see that the weight of waiting time in System time increases and hence we get a lower correlation between system time and service time.

  5. Autocorrelation of waiting time: We observe that for high RHO ( 0.8 and 0.9) we see that the the autocoreelation of the waiting times is very high for until a lag of around 10(for RHO=0.8) and even 20(for RHO=0.9). This means that the waiting time of at least 5 consecutive packets is more or less the same or that if the packet before the observed packet had to wait, it is very likely that the observed packet will also have to wait for a similar amount of time because the server is busy more often when the RHO is high. When RHO is low, the packets are processed quickly and hence packets usually dont have to wait at all and hence the waiting times of consecutive packets will not have any relation and the waiting time will anyway be close to 0 as mentioned above.

Task 4.4.3: Visualization of Covariance/Correlation¶

From the first plot (Service Time vs IAT) we see that the points are more or less randomly distributed and more points are closer to the origin. This is because (as explained in point 2 of task 4.4.2) the IAT and Service time are both a result of our two implemented RNGs and hence they will be uncorrelated. The values are closer to the origin with some outliers in father away is because of the nature of exponentially distributed random variables. From the second plot we see the dropping correlation between serving time and system time as RHO increases. The first plot has very high correlation as can be seen from the the fact that most points are ordered on a line. The points get mosre disorderely as we increase RHO.

Task 4.4.4: Auto Correlation¶

In Task 4.3.3 we plot the Autocorrelation of waiting times for 100 and 10000 packets respectively. As explained in point 5 of Task 4.4.2 the Autocorelation stays high for longer lag as we increase RHO because consecutive packets are expected to wait for similar time as RHO increases. This can be seen from the slope of the curves in the plot, the drop is more gradual as we increse RHO. We see that the curves are 'smoother' when we have simulated for more number of packets and this is due the the fact that we observed longer during a steady state phase. The autocorelation even drops to a -0.3 when we simulate for 100 packets which is spurious and does not represent steady state behaviour.
